About Me

I am currently pursuing a BA LLB at Government Law College, Mumbai, with a growing interest in legal research and academic writing. My current areas of interest include technology law, public policy, governance, and socio-legal issues, though I am ultimately drawn more by meaningful questions than by any single field.

What I enjoy most about research is the process of inquiry. I enjoy understanding why things are the way they are, examining assumptions, and following evidence wherever it leads. I see hypotheses as starting points for investigation rather than conclusions to defend, and I value careful methodology, precision, and intellectual honesty throughout the research process.

Academic History

An early interest in corporate and commercial law led to the study of the Company Secretary curriculum, where the focus gradually shifted from learning legal provisions to understanding the reasoning behind them. Questions about why institutions are designed as they are, what objectives legal rules seek to achieve, and how those rules perform in practice became more compelling than the memorization of doctrine itself. That transition ultimately shaped a broader interest in legal research, with an emphasis on institutional design, governance, technology law, and evidence-based policy analysis.

Current Directions

Current research focuses on three broad questions:

  • How should legal and regulatory frameworks adapt to emerging technologies such as cryptocurrencies and digital assets while preserving legal certainty and institutional legitimacy?
  • To what extent do existing legal structures, such as the Limited Liability Partnership framework, achieve their intended objectives when examined through empirical evidence?
  • How can legal institutions reconcile competing normative obligations with practical constraints, particularly in areas such as asylum, constitutional governance, and public policy?
