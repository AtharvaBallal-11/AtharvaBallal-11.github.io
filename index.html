Atharva Ballal

I am currently pursuing a BA LLB at Government Law College, Mumbai, with a growing interest in legal research and academic writing. My current areas of interest include technology law, public policy, governance, and socio-legal issues, though I am ultimately drawn more by meaningful questions than by any single field.

What I enjoy most about research is the process of inquiry. I enjoy understanding why things are the way they are, examining assumptions, and following evidence wherever it leads. I see hypotheses as starting points for investigation rather than conclusions to defend, and I value careful methodology, precision, and intellectual honesty throughout the research process.

§ Current Work

Current work focuses on the development of a Notified Groups Framework (NGF) for asylum law, examining how humanitarian obligations can be reconciled with finite state capacity through a structured, principled model of protection.

Selected Work

The Emergence of Intimate Surveillance: Mandatory Live-In Registration and the Constitutional Limits of State Knowledge

Submitted for Publication

Analyzes how state-level mandatory live-in registration laws in Uttarakhand, Gujarat, and Assam interact with constitutional principles of privacy and autonomy, arguing that the new surveillance-oriented model departs from protections established through judicial precedent.

Regulating the Gateways: Sovereign Enforcement, Asymmetric Adoption, and the Structural Limits of India's Digital Asset Regime

Submitted for Publication

Develops a gateway-centric theory of digital asset regulation, arguing that India's current framework creates an asymmetry between enforcement and consumer protection, and proposes a comparative model informed by the EU, Singapore, and the UAE.
