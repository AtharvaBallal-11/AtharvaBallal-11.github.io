Empirical Assessment of the Limited Liability Partnership Act, 2008

Status: Archived
Atharva Ballal | Completed: June 2026

Abstract

This project evaluates the operational, governance, and structural effectiveness of the Limited Liability Partnership Act, 2008 by surveying 24 corporate compliance and legal practitioners across India. The central finding is that the principal obstacles facing LLPs arise less from the statutory framework itself than from its implementation. Respondents consistently identified administrative delays in regulatory filings, compliance procedures surrounding capital changes, and institution-specific practices — particularly in banking — as the primary sources of operational friction. While the LLP Act provides a robust legal foundation, these implementation bottlenecks systematically reduce the practical benefits intended by the legislation.
