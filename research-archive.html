Regulating the Gateways: Sovereign Enforcement, Asymmetric Adoption, and the Structural Limits of India's Digital Asset Regime

Submitted

This paper proposes gateway-centric sovereign regulation as the only technically coherent framework for governing decentralised digital asset markets, arguing that effective regulation must operate at institutional gateways rather than the protocol layer. It critiques India's regime of asymmetric adoption and draws on models in Singapore, the UAE, and the EU to propose a framework for India organised around licensing, statutory custody, functional unbundling, and fiscal rationalisation.……

The Emergence of Intimate Surveillance: Mandatory Live-In Registration and the Constitutional Limits of State Knowledge

Submitted

For over two decades, Indian law approached live-in relationships through a remedial paradigm, but emerging state legislation in Uttarakhand, Gujarat, and Assam replaces this with a surveillance model built from compelled intimate disclosure, police notification, and criminal enforcement. The paper argues this expansion of monitoring operates in tension with the constitutional protections courts have developed over two decades, and concludes that the surveillance model is fundamentally in tension with the judicial approach.……

The Grammar of Authority: Law, Morality, and the Architecture of Power

Submitted

This essay argues that morality is not an external standard against which law is measured, but the internal language through which institutional power stabilises itself, challenging both natural law theory and legal positivism. Addressing objections concerning self-sacrifice, justification, and atrocity, it concludes that moral frameworks emerge within particular institutional arrangements, and asks what conditions shape those frameworks and whose interests they serve.……

The Notified Groups Framework: A Constitutional Alternative to Uniform Asylum Law in India

Working Draft

This paper proposes the Notified Groups Framework as a constitutional alternative to a Uniform Asylum Law in India, arguing that a judicially enforceable UAL assumes administrative capacity, geopolitical insulation, and fiscal capability that the Indian state does not presently possess at scale. The NGF features parliamentary scrutiny, a two-tier protection architecture preserving Article 21 rights, and event-specific, time-bound classification to satisfy Article 14 scrutiny while also addressing climate-induced displacement.……
