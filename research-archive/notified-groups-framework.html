The Notified Groups Framework: A Constitutional Alternative to Uniform Asylum Law in India

Status: Working Draft
Atharva Ballal | 2026

Abstract

India's refugee governance operates through executive discretion rather than codified law, generating persistent demands for a Uniform Asylum Law (UAL). This paper proposes a different instrument: the Notified Groups Framework (NGF), a statutory mechanism grounded in Union List legislative competence and Section 3(2) of the Foreigners Act, 1946, under which Parliament codifies a bounded set of refugee protections while the Central Executive designates beneficiary groups through gazette notification. The paper argues, through doctrinal analysis of primary constitutional sources and selective comparative review, that a judicially enforceable UAL assumes administrative capacity, geopolitical insulation, and fiscal absorptive capability that the Indian state does not presently possess at scale.

Three structural features differentiate the NGF from both the status quo and a UAL: a parliamentary framework that creates accountability costs for executive non-notification through mandatory reason-giving and parliamentary scrutiny; a two-tier protection architecture preserving the Article 21 procedural floor for all persons regardless of notification status; and an event-specific, time-bound classification mechanism that satisfies Article 14 scrutiny without replicating the constitutional vulnerabilities of the Citizenship Amendment Act, 2019. The NGF is also designed to accommodate climate-induced displacement through prospective notification, an increasingly urgent consideration given the Teitiota trajectory in international human rights law.
