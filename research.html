Active Research

This page provides an overview of my current research activities, including working papers, developing ideas, and ongoing investigations. It is updated periodically to reflect my evolving research interests and areas of inquiry.

Working Papers & Drafts

  • A Notified Groups Framework for Refugee Governance in India

    Status: Work in Progress
    Field: Refugee Law

    Examines India's refugee governance through the lens of executive discretion and statutory design. The paper argues that the absence of a comprehensive refugee law reflects a structurally flexible model of governance rather than a simple legislative omission, and proposes a Notified Groups Framework (NGF) that preserves executive discretion while providing statutory rights and legal certainty for recognized refugee communities.

  • Regulating the Gateways: Sovereign Enforcement, Asymmetric Adoption, and the Structural Limits of India's Digital Asset Regime

    Status: Submitted for Publication
    Field: Financial Regulation

    Examines how sovereign states can effectively regulate decentralized digital asset markets through institutional gateways such as exchanges, custodians, and fiat conversion points. The paper develops a gateway-centric theory of digital asset regulation, argues that India's current framework creates an asymmetry between enforcement and consumer protection, and proposes a comparative regulatory framework informed by models from the EU, Singapore, and the UAE.

  • The Emergence of Intimate Surveillance: Mandatory Live-In Registration and the Constitutional Limits of State Knowledge

    Status: Submitted for Publication
    Field: Constitutional Law

    Examines the shift in India's regulation of live-in relationships from a remedial legal framework to one centered on mandatory registration and state knowledge. The paper analyzes how emerging state legislation in Uttarakhand, Gujarat, and Assam interacts with constitutional principles of privacy and autonomy, arguing that the new surveillance-oriented model departs significantly from the protections developed through judicial precedent over the past two decades.

Methodological Philosophy

My research begins with the assumption that legal problems are rarely isolated. I approach each question by identifying the institutional, economic, and technological systems within which legal rules operate, before examining how doctrine, legislation, and judicial interpretation shape their outcomes. Where appropriate, I complement doctrinal analysis with empirical inquiry to test whether legal frameworks function in practice as they are intended to in theory. Throughout my work, I aim to frame questions precisely, engage seriously with competing viewpoints, and distinguish clearly between normative arguments, empirical observations, and legal analysis. I believe rigorous scholarship is built not only on sound reasoning, but also on methodological transparency and intellectual humility.
