Programmatic Lending and UCC Article 12: Reconciling Code with Commercial Practice

Status: Under Review
Atharva Ballal | Last Updated: June 27, 2026

Abstract

The adoption of the 2022 amendments to the Uniform Commercial Code introduces Article 12, regulating "Controllable Electronic Records" (CERs) such as cryptocurrencies and tokenized assets. Programmatic lending protocols execute collateral liquidation automatically through pre-written smart contract logic. This paper analyzes whether such autonomous liquidations satisfy the "commercial reasonableness" standard under UCC § 9-610. By exploring how code-based auction designs align with commercial standards, we propose a framework for integrating protocol execution with traditional secured transaction doctrines.

Research Question

Can autonomous, code-directed collateral liquidations initiated by decentralized lending protocols meet the statutory requirements of "commercial reasonableness" under UCC Article 9, and how does the "control" requirement of UCC § 12-105 impact the analysis?

Methods

This study employs a doctrinal legal methodology combined with functional software analysis. We map the statutory criteria of UCC §§ 9-610, 9-627, and 12-105 onto the automated liquidation flows of major decentralized lending protocols (e.g., Aave, MakerDAO). We evaluate protocol codebases to verify how they manage transfer rights and user authentication relative to the statutory concept of "exclusive control."

Current Progress

The manuscript is currently under peer review at a specialized technology law journal. We are preparing responses to reviewer comments, particularly focusing on how programmatic Dutch auctions handle sudden market liquidity shocks.
