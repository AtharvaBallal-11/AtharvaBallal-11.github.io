The Uniform Commercial Code and Algorithmic Collateral: Security Interests in Decentralized Ledger Protocols

Status: Published
Atharva Ballal | Published: January 2025

Abstract

This paper investigates how security interests in decentralized, algorithmically managed ledger protocol pools were perfected prior to the introduction of UCC Article 12. It reviews the limits of possession and control paradigms under traditional Article 9 rules, examining how lenders navigated perfection via filing vs. control over security entitlements. We propose structural guidelines for integrating automated liquidation systems with traditional private law doctrines.

Research Question

How did commercial lenders achieve legal perfection of security interests in crypto-assets under the pre-2022 amendment Uniform Commercial Code, and what structural friction did they encounter?

Methods

We employ doctrinal analysis of the Uniform Commercial Code (specifically Articles 8 and 9) alongside structured interviews with 14 commercial practitioners specializing in secured lending against digital assets.
